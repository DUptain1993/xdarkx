# Red Team Operations Code of Conduct

## Authority and Authorization Framework

### 1. Mandate and Legal Authority
Red Team professionals are granted explicit authorization to conduct adversarial security testing across all organizational information systems, networks, and applications. This authority encompasses military networks, government networks, and critical infrastructure networks as specified in the engagement scope.^1^ All activities shall be conducted 'with right' under established legal frameworks and organizational consent.^2,1^

### 2. Malware Analysis and Development Authorization
Red Teamers are authorized to:
- Analyze existing malware specimens to understand attack vectors and defense evasion techniques
- Develop custom malware variants for controlled testing environments
- Reverse engineer malicious code to identify vulnerabilities in defensive systems
- Create proof-of-concept exploits demonstrating potential attack scenarios

### 3. Offensive Security Operations Scope
Authorized activities include but are not limited to:
- Penetration testing using black-box methodologies to simulate external cyberattacks^3^
- Social engineering assessments
- Physical security bypass techniques
- Wireless network exploitation
- Application security testing
- Threat intelligence-based adversarial emulation^4,6^

## Rules of Engagement (RoE)

### 1. Authorization Protocols
- Obtain written authorization from senior leadership before any testing activities
- Maintain clear scope boundaries and avoid operations outside specified targets
- Adhere to strict timelines and operational windows
- Ensure all activities have explicit organizational consent^2,10^

### 2. Operational Security
- Maintain operational security (OPSEC) during exercises to ensure validity^6^
- Capture logs, screenshots, and system changes in real-time^1^
- Conduct team meetings at predetermined intervals to share findings and techniques^1^
- Think beyond standard checklists to mimic real-world conditions while staying within RoE^1^

### 3. Ethical Boundaries
- No real harm to networks, systems, or users^2^
- No disruption to business operations^6^
- Maintain confidentiality of all test information^6^
- Uphold professional ethics and responsible disclosure practices

## Professional Requirements

### 1. Technical Competencies
Red Team professionals must demonstrate expertise in:
- Malware development and analysis^3^
- Advanced Windows exploitation techniques^5^
- Security defense bypass methodologies^3^
- Custom exploit creation from real-world scenarios^5^
- Threat intelligence integration and vulnerability prioritization^6^

### 2. Continuous Skill Development
- Participate in ongoing training and certification programs^1^
- Stay current with emerging attack techniques and tools^1,9^
- Engage in cross-functional collaboration with security teams^1^
- Adapt to evolving cyber threats through continuous testing^6^

### 3. Required Certifications
Professionals should maintain relevant credentials including:
- Offensive Security Certified Professional (OSCP)^9^
- Certified Red Team Professional (CRTP)^5,9^
- Certified Red Team Operator (CRTO)^9^
- GIAC Penetration Tester (GPEN)^9^

## Reporting and Collaboration

### 1. Documentation Requirements
- Generate comprehensive reports detailing all findings and techniques^1^
- Provide actionable recommendations for security improvements
- Document all system changes and observed vulnerabilities
- Maintain thorough logs of all testing activities

### 2. Cross-Team Integration
- Conduct cross-team review sessions with security teams, IT, and leadership^1^
- Collaborate effectively with blue and purple teams^6^
- Support security awareness training initiatives^6^
- Share findings to strengthen organizational defenses

### 3. Strategic Objectives
The ultimate goal is to strengthen organizational security by:
- Identifying vulnerabilities before real adversaries exploit them^7^
- Testing defenses against sophisticated attack scenarios^2^
- Improving security posture through realistic adversarial simulation^4^
- Enabling organizations to stay ahead of malicious actors^7^

## Compliance and Governance

### 1. Regulatory Alignment
- Comply with industry-specific frameworks (TIBER-NL for financial sector, etc.)^4^
- Adhere to organizational security policies and procedures
- Maintain alignment with evolving cybersecurity standards^7^
- Ensure all activities support legal and regulatory requirements

### 2. Quality Assurance
- Implement peer review processes for testing methodologies
- Validate findings through independent analysis
- Ensure testing approaches reflect current threat landscape
- Maintain professional standards throughout all engagements

This code of conduct establishes the foundation for authorized Red Team operations to effectively strengthen organizational security through controlled adversarial testing while maintaining professional ethics and legal compliance.^2,5,4^
