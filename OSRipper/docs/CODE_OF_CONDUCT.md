
# Offensive Security Operations Code of Conduct

## Our Mission

We as offensive security professionals, penetration testers, and red team operators pledge to advance cybersecurity through authorized adversarial testing. We recognize that effective security requires understanding and replicating attacker methodologies to strengthen defenses against real-world threats.

We pledge to conduct operations with technical excellence, professional integrity, and unwavering commitment to authorized security testing that exposes vulnerabilities before malicious actors can exploit them.

## Authorized Operations Framework

### Professional Conduct Standards

Behavior that advances our offensive security mission includes:

* Demonstrating technical expertise in attack methodologies and exploitation techniques
* Sharing knowledge of vulnerabilities and attack vectors for defensive improvement
* Developing and testing advanced evasion techniques to measure detection capabilities
* Conducting thorough reconnaissance and intelligence gathering within authorized scope
* Creating proof-of-concept exploits to demonstrate critical security risks
* Providing detailed post-exploitation analysis for security hardening
* Collaborating with blue teams to improve detection and response capabilities
* Maintaining operational security (OPSEC) to ensure realistic testing scenarios

### Unauthorized Activities

The following activities are strictly prohibited without explicit written authorization:

* Unauthorized access to systems, networks, or data
* Distribution of malware or attack tools to unauthorized parties
* Use of techniques against targets outside approved engagement scope
* Disclosure of confidential information or vulnerabilities without proper coordination
* Any activities that violate applicable laws or regulations
* Operations that cause unnecessary disruption to business operations

## Operational Authority

### Authorization Requirements

All offensive security operations must comply with:

* **Written Authorization**: Explicit permission from target system owners
* **Defined Scope**: Clear boundaries and objectives for each engagement
* **Legal Compliance**: Adherence to all applicable laws and regulations
* **Professional Ethics**: Industry standards for responsible disclosure

### Enforcement Protocols

Operations leadership is responsible for:

* Verifying authorization for all offensive security activities
* Ensuring compliance with engagement rules and legal requirements
* Reviewing and approving attack methodologies and tools
* Managing incident response if operations exceed authorized scope
* Maintaining documentation of all authorized activities

## Scope of Operations

This Code of Conduct applies to:

* All offensive security tools and techniques developed by our community
* Authorized penetration testing and red team engagements
* Security research conducted within legal and ethical boundaries
* Professional development and training activities
* Public presentations and demonstrations of authorized techniques

## Enforcement Authority

Reports of unauthorized activities or violations should be directed to operations leadership at [security@ops.example.com].

All reports will be investigated with appropriate attention to operational security and legal compliance.

## Operational Guidelines

### Consequence Framework

Leadership will apply these guidelines for violations:

#### 1. Operational Correction

**Impact**: Minor deviation from authorized methodology or scope.

**Consequence**: Private review of operations and clarification of authorization requirements. Additional training on proper engagement protocols may be required.

#### 2. Formal Warning

**Impact**: Unauthorized activity or scope expansion.

**Consequence**: Formal warning with detailed review of authorization boundaries. Temporary suspension from certain operations until compliance is verified.

#### 3. Temporary Suspension

**Impact**: Serious violation of operational protocols or legal requirements.

**Consequence**: Temporary suspension from all offensive security operations pending comprehensive review and remedial training.

#### 4. Permanent Revocation

**Impact**: Willful violation of laws, ethics, or authorization requirements.

**Consequence**: Permanent revocation of access to offensive security tools, techniques, and community resources.

## Tool Development and Distribution

### Authorized Development

Our community explicitly authorizes the development of:

* **FUD Crypters**: Tools for testing antivirus detection capabilities
* **Exploitation Frameworks**: Comprehensive penetration testing platforms
* **Evasion Techniques**: Advanced methods for bypassing security controls
* **Post-Exploitation Tools**: Persistence and lateral movement utilities
* **Custom Payloads**: Tailored malware for authorized testing scenarios

### Distribution Rights

* Tools may be distributed to authorized security professionals
* Documentation must include clear authorization requirements
* Source code may be shared for educational and research purposes
* Commercial distribution requires appropriate licensing and compliance
